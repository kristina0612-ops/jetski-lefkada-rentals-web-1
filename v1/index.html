Chapter 07
The small print, big

Questions we love.

01

Do I need a boat license?

For our fleet, no separate boat license is required if you ride under our supervision and complete the on-site safety briefing. You must be at least 18 and present a valid passport or ID at pickup. If you hold a Greek boat license, even better - we'll get you on the water faster.

02

Is insurance included?

Yes. Every rental is covered by third-party liability insurance up to €700.000 per incident, as required by Greek Law 4926/2022. Your own safety is not insured by the rental - we strongly recommend your personal travel insurance covers watersports. We can recommend day-policies if needed.

03

What happens if the weather is bad?

Greek Coast Guard forbids operation above Beaufort 5. If your booking falls on a bad-weather day we reschedule for free or refund 100%. We check the forecast every morning at 07:00 and will WhatsApp you by 08:00 if there's any doubt.

04

Can I cancel my booking?

Free cancellation up to 48 hours before your start time. Between 48h and 24h we refund 50%. Less than 24h is non-refundable unless the weather grounds us. Deposit-only bookings (30%) follow the same rules.

05

Where do I meet David?

You receive exact GPS coordinates with your booking confirmation. Our main dock is in Nidri, on the east coast. Parking is free. It's a 10-minute walk from the village center.

06

Can I bring passengers?

Yes - each jetski is rated for 2 or 3 people depending on the model. All passengers must wear the life jacket we provide. Kids from 8 years old are welcome as passengers with a parent or guardian.

07

How far can I ride?

Greek Coast Guard rules: max 700 meters from the shore, never inside swim-zone buoys. Within those rules you have plenty of coastline to explore - we'll hand you a printed map of the best coves and bays.

08

What if I crash or damage the jetski?

You leave a security deposit at arrival (€300-€500 depending on the model). Insurance covers third-party damage. Damage to the jetski itself from gross negligence is your responsibility. The briefing exists exactly to avoid this.
